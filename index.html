Email: sai.neha.santpur@cern.ch

My research focuses on experimental particle physics, particularly search for beyond Standard Model physics involving long-lived particles. I work on searches for long-lived particles (LLPs) using the CMS experiment at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). I am currently working on searches involving delayed jets, which utilize jet timing in the CMS Electromagnetic Calorimeter to tag the signal. Dedicated triggers to target delayed jets were introduced in Run-3, increasing sensitivity relative to Run-2. I am currently the Trigger covnener for the Exotics group in the CMS collaboration.

I also work on searches for millicharged particles (mCPs) predicted in dark-sector theories which can provide a viable dark matter candidate. The LHC could produce mCPs through Drell-Yan, meson and other production modes. However, dedicated detectors are required to capture their small signatures. I work on milliQan and FORMOSA experiments at CERN, which provide world-leading sensitivity to mCPs over a wide range of masses and charges.
